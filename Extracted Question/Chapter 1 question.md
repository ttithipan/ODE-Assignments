## Assignment 1
Write a differential equation modeling each situation below:
1. The time rate of change of the velocity $v(t)$ of a coasting motorboat is proportional to the square of $v$.
2. The acceleration $\frac{dv}{dt}$ of a Lamborghini is proportional to the difference between 250 km/h and the velocity of the car.
3. In a city having a fixed population $P$, the rate of change of the number $N(t)$ of people who have heard a rumor is proportional to the number of people who have not yet heard it.
4. In a city with a fixed population $P$, the rate of change of the number $N(t)$ of people infected with a contagious disease is proportional to the product of the number of infected and uninfected individuals.

## Assignment 2
1. Show that the general (one-parameter) solution of the differential equation $\frac{dx}{dt}=kx^{2}$ is given by $x(t)=\frac{1}{(C-k)t}$, where $C$ is an arbitrary constant and $k$ is a constant. Determine by inspection a solution of the initial value problem $\frac{dx}{dt}=kx^{2}, x(0)=0$.
2. A rodent population $P(t)$ satisfies the differential equation $\frac{dP}{dt}=kP^{2}$. Initially, $P(0)=2$. When $P=10$, the rate is $\frac{dP}{dt}=1$. Use this to: Determine the constant $k$. Use the result of the previous problem to compute the time required for the population to grow to 100 rodents and to 1000 rodents. Interpret the result. What happens to the population as time increases?
3. The velocity $v(t)$ of a coasting motorboat satisfies $\frac{dv}{dt}=-kv^{2}$. Given $v(0)=10\text{ m/s}$ and $\frac{dv}{dt}=-1$ when $v=5\text{ m/s}$. Find the constant $k$. Find the time required for the velocity to decrease to 1 m/s and to 0.1 m/s. What is the behavior of the velocity as time goes on? When does the boat stop?

## Assignment 6
1. A river is 1 mile wide with midstream velocity $v_{0}=9\text{ mi/h}$, and the banks are located at $x=\pm 0.5\text{ mi}$. The velocity profile of the river is quadratic: $v_{R}(x)=v_{0}(1-\frac{x^{2}}{a^{2}})$, $a=0.5$. A swimmer begins at $(x,y)=(-0.5,0)$ and swims due east (perpendicular to the banks) with constant speed $v_{S}$. What must the swimmer's speed $v_{S}$ be so that they drift only 1 mile downstream upon reaching the opposite bank at $x=0.5$?
2. The swimmer's speed is fixed at $v_{S}=3\text{ mi/h}$ but the river velocity is a fourth-degree function: $v_{R}(x)=v_{0}(1-\frac{x^{4}}{a^{4}})$, $a=0.5$. The swimmer starts again at $(-0.5,0)$ and swims due east. How far downstream does the swimmer drift before reaching $x=0.5$?

4. A driver claims he was going $25\text{ mi/h}$ before braking. Tests show the car stops in 45 ft at that speed. But his skid marks at the scene measure 210 ft. Assuming constant deceleration, what was his actual speed before braking?
